Project

Overview

We want to create the ultimate lightweight overlay for playing music on Spotify.

The overlay will be bound to a hotkey that will bring up an informative minimalist window in the upper left corner of your screen. This window will have intuitive button-based controls that allow for rapid song selection and effortless volume adjustment, among other things—all without causing any disruption to your current focus. Application will also have in-built user stat tracking. Once installed, the program will launch as a background process on windows start-up and fully initialise when Spotify is open and playing. Since the goal is to be as unobtrusive as possible, this will have almost zero performance impact.

Music is one of the common pleasures we all share, one way or another; almost everyone listens to music in some form. Today, Spotify alone has 406 million users, with over 25% of Americans reporting that they use Spotify monthly. That's a lot of songs being skipped, yet we can't help feeling like the action is clunky and distracting. If we want more choice over what track to play next, you have to stop what you're doing, open the application, and sift through unorganised playlists until you find something that tickles your fancy. What if there was a convenient overlay that knew what you wanted to play ahead of time, didn't involve alt-tabbing and gave more control than the original service, all without breaking focus on your workflow or gameplay. That's what we want to achieve.

With such a large user count there is always going to be competitors, although if there are they are making themselves hard to find because we found very little in the way of a direct competitor. What sets our project apart from the others is our customizable display feature allowing users to be as minimalist as they want to be, and the most important difference is our user based stat tracking, along with the exciting things that are planned to work alongside this feature in the future. We believe that with the features we have, and the ideas being planned for the future we will be able to stay at the front of the pack, delivering a unique way to manage music streaming while on the grind.

Aims

The main aim of this project is to create the ultimate overlay for playing music on Spotify. Many PC users enjoy listening to music while working, studying, gaming or just simply browsing on a PC. While this can currently be done via playing music through Spotify, there is no option to change the playlist unless you reopen the tab. The aim of the project is to allow users to change their music choice, as well as adjust volume with the press of a button.

The first goal is to make the program with the code, which is the most important part of the project, there is no project without the needed code. This part should have priority over the rest of the goals. Ensuring that the Tekore Library can be used with our coding language of Python allows us to move forward with the project.

Another goal is to implement the code to be used within other applications, ensuring it can be used how we intend for it to be used, as well as ensuring that certain programs or games will allow the project to be used during gameplay/work. We need to ensure that our project can work under different PC applications firewalls.

Finally, there is the goal of ensuring when pressing the needed buttons to control the overlay, that these do not interrupt with the users open application. Using keys that are not normally used by the everyday user can assist in this goal, allowing this to not be a major goal for the project.

Plans and Progress

Given that our goal is to create a lightweight Spotify overlay this project doesn't contain a massive amount of features, it displays a small window at the top of the screen along with mappable hotkeys to adjust volume and change songs. We are also excited about the implementation of an expandable window that will contain user stat tracking, letting the user see how much music they have been listening to, most listened to genre or artist, songs and more, without having to wait for the once-a-year Spotify wrap up. Our core design goal from the start, was to create a lightweight, informative, and as unobtrusive as possible overlay, allowing user to flick through there Spotify library without losing focus on the task at hand, whilst having the option to display detailed statistics about their music listening history.

At present our project is still in the very early development stage, we have developed a proof-of-concept animation to visually display the direction we are heading with our overlay. Below are some screenshots taken from said animation, to see full animation refer to our video presentation.

But let's go back and talk about how we got here, and where we plan to go moving forward.
With all members of our group being avid music listeners through Spotify, along with the reported 180 million other Spotify subscribers, (Spotify users - subscribers in 2022 | Statista, 2022), not just users but paying customers worldwide, we felt that our frustrations, along with many of the growing concerns and frustrations around the applications clunky interface from users everywhere, was something worth spending time on to see if we could make an improvement. In line with our design philosophy, we sat down to discuss, “what is the bare minimum number of features we need for this overlay to function but remain lightweight?”.

With this is mind we landed on the following:

  • All actions must be hotkey mappable
  • Pause/play
  • Cycle through library or playlist tracks
  • Volume adjustment
  • Ability to scrub through tracks
  • User statistics
  • Minimalistic visual display of the information

While it was tempting to get carried away here adding more and more features, we opted to stay with a smaller more achievable goal that left room for expansion in the future.

When it came down to deciding what software and programming was going to be needed to implement our features in the way we had envisioned, and given the group relatively novice level of knowledge around programming languages, we anticipate this list is likely going to be added to as the project moves forward. We decided to use Python as our language of choice as some of the group had some proficiency in it, along with a few libraries being, Tekore, pygame, tkinter and pywin32. We believe this set of libraries will cover the implementation of the features we are after, but again we are prepared for this to change as the project evolve, and are confident we can overcome these obstacles.

Given our limited knowledge around the backend side of things we opted for focusing on the frontend design before diving into the coding portion of the overlay. Doing so has allowed us to create a first visual mock-up of the design giving a solid foundation to build upon visually and a clear target to pursue. Which brings us back to the present stage of the project, so what's next?

One section has been alluded to, but not shown, user statistics. This feature was a late addition to the project, but one that we felt had a place within our application, while not a tool that is for everyone, having the ability to track listening hours or what genre/artist those hours were spent on, was something that got our team excited. Bunch of statistic nerds. This will likely be our next course of action, to design a simplistic expandable window for displaying user statistics that still holds true to our original design goals, after this we move into the coding phase.

Coding will undoubtedly be the lengthiest phase of the project, due to inexperience there will be quite the learning curve as we move through integrating the various features into an application. But as stated earlier we are confident that with some experimentation and practise, we can overcome these hurdles to create an eye-pleasing, more productive, streamlined solution to alt-tabbing away from work to handle music.

Some of the features that arose throughout the planning process were a leader board system working alongside user statistics to compete with friends, variant style options for the overlay itself beyond the default options like border style options or complete themes. While we are excited to look further into these features, this would be reserved for future versions of the application as these types of features generally are used by fairly niche portion of the population.

Roles

We have not defined specific roles in our group, being all new to the field of IT we felt it better to all remain the same hat to better our collective knowledge across every aspect of the project. While there were no defined roles, all group members held certain strengths, with task delegation being handled by assigning jobs to those had the most confidence in the given task. Working this way allowed better communication between group members, as well as the ability to learn from one another in area's that group members hadn't been exposed to before, it also fostered a healthy environment for discussing topics none of us were fluent in, brainstorming, bouncing idea around and learning together.

Scope and Limits

The priority of the project is to produce our Spotify Overlay using the Tekore Library, along with our chosen coding language Python. The scope includes the overlay that works with keys on all keyboards, allowing the user to switch songs and playlists whenever suits them, that works on all major PC applications, including Microsoft applications, internet browsers, emails etc. The limits include the mentioned feature of user statistics, as well as a leader board system that works alongside other users. Neither of these are priorities in the alluded time frame for the project.

Risks

Most of the risks for our overlay project are those surrounding the software required to build the application. As we are using multiple python associated libraries, there is a definite risk of these libraries not being able to fulfil the requirements that is needed to complete this project. There is also a risk of becoming obsolete due to constantly evolving hardware, in particular keyboards, as a lot of these products feature built-in keys for pause/play, skipping tracks and volume controls, rendering the need for additional keybinds moot. The overlay GUI itself to visibly see current track choice is also at risk of being made obsolete, given the general demographic of people that would be interested in such an overlay is gamers/heavy IT users, it is very commonplace now to have multiple displays, this coupled with in-built options on keyboards might see this project fall short of our anticipated need.
